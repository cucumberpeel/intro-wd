Baking is definitely an important source of stress relief for me. I mostly use other people's recipes, but there are a few things that I've baked so many times that I've adjusted my own recipe as a result. Basic chocolate chip cookies are one of those items.

Before you begin, do *not* preheat your oven ok

Recipe

I forgot how many cookies this makes. I think it's 36

    Ingredients

  • 1 stick unsalted butter

  • 3/4 cup brown sugar

  • 1/2 cup white sugar

  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

  • 1 egg + 1 egg yolk

  • 1 1/4 cup flour

  • 1/2 tsp baking soda

  • 1 pinch of salt

  • 1 bar chocolate (dark or bittersweet recommended)

  • 1/8 cup chocolate chunks

    Instructions

  1. Melt butter in pan, stirring frequently. When brown bits start to appear, remove from heat and cool to room temperature. When cooled, add 2 tbsp room temperature water to make up for lost volume.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk brown butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until thoroughly combined. Add vanilla extract and eggs, whisking until just uniform to avoid overmixing eggs.

  3. In a separate bowl, sift flour, baking soda, and salt. Mix until combined, then add to wet ingredients.

  4. Fold dry ingredients into wet ingredients with a spatula.

  5. When combined, chop chocolate bar and fold all chocolate into dough with a spatula.

  6. Chill for minimum 1 hour, or overnight for best results.

  7. When ready to bake, preheat oven to 350 F.

  8. Bake for 10 minutes at 350 F.

  9. Let cool for minimum 20 minutes before enjoying.
